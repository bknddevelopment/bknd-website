You count a signed contract after an in-person estimate. That's two completely different games.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Geography limits everything.</strong> You can't scale by reaching more people. A viral post doesn't help when your trucks only drive 30 miles.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Seasonality hits hard.</strong> HVAC companies live and die by weather.         <div class="flex items-start gap-3">
          <span class="text-amber-500 text-xl">‚ö†Ô∏è</span>
          <p class="text-[#1D1D1F] m-0">If you skip this section and jump straight to "how do I get more leads," you'll waste money. I'm putting this first because nothing else matters without it.

      </div><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">I've audited hundreds of contractor marketing setups. Maybe 5% had tracking that could actually tell them which marketing generated which jobs.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most have Google Analytics installed. Almost none have it configured to measure what matters.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">GA4 Setup That Works for Services</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Standard GA4 tracks page views. Useless. You need it tracking what matters for <a href="/services/analytics" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">proper analytics setup</a>:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Phone call clicks from your website. This requires setting up event tracking for tel: links. If your developer tells you this is hard, get a new developer. If someone reads 75% of your roofing page, they're a warmer lead than someone who bounced after 3 seconds. Treat them differently.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Here's the GA4 event setup you actually need:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Create custom events for: phone_click, form_submit, chat_initiated, and quote_request. Tag each with the traffic source, landing page, and service category. This takes about two hours to set up properly. Those two hours will save you from making $50,000 in bad marketing decisions.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Call Tracking Implementation</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This is where most contractors lose the plot entirely.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You need a call tracking system. CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, WhatConverts‚Äîpick one. They all work. The point is getting a unique phone number for each marketing channel so you know what's generating calls.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The setup: Your Google Ads get one tracking number. Your organic search traffic sees a different number on your website (dynamic number insertion). Your Google Business Profile gets another. Your Facebook ads get another.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Now when someone calls, you know exactly which marketing brought them in. Revolutionary, right? They're just guessing.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">One warning: don't use too many tracking numbers or you'll dilute your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency for local SEO. </p>
        </div>
      </div><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Content & Website Optimization</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your website exists to convert visitors into leads. That's it. Everything else is secondary. This is where <a href="/services/cro" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">conversion rate optimization</a> becomes essential.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Pages That Convert for Service Businesses</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You need these pages, optimized for both search and conversion:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Homepage: Clear value proposition, primary service areas, trust signals (reviews, certifications, years in business), and obvious contact options. Don't bury the phone number.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Individual service pages: One page per major service. Not a generic "services" page. "Roof Replacement" gets its own page. "Roof Repair" gets its own page. "Storm Damage Repair" gets its own page. Each page targets specific keywords and addresses specific concerns.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Location pages: If you serve multiple cities, each city gets a page. "Roofing Contractor in Round Rock" is a different page than "Roofing Contractor in Cedar Park." Yes, it's more work. Yes, it matters.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">About/Team page: Real photos of real people. Bios that establish credibility. This is where you build the trust that converts browsers into callers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Reviews/testimonials page: Aggregated proof that you do good work. Embed your Google reviews, add photos of completed projects, include video testimonials if you have them.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">The One Thing Most Contractor Websites Get Wrong</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Buried contact information.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">I've seen contractor websites where you have to scroll through three screens of content to find a phone number. I've seen sites where "Contact Us" is a tiny link in the footer. I've seen sites where the only way to reach the company is through a contact form hidden on a separate page.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your phone number should be visible without scrolling on every page. Click-to-call on mobile. Sticky header with contact info that follows the user.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">People searching for home services often have urgent needs. Make it stupidly easy to call you. If someone lands on your site and can't immediately figure out how to contact you, they're gone.</p><div class="my-8 p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-[#00D4FF]/10 to-[#00D4FF]/5 border-l-4 border-[#00D4FF] rounded-r-lg">
        <div class="text-4xl lg:text-5xl font-bold text-[#00D4FF] mb-2">70%+</div>
        <p class="text-[#1D1D1F] text-sm lg:text-base m-0">of local searches happen on phones. If your site is slow or clunky on mobile, you're hemorrhaging leads. Mobile experience matters more than desktop.
</p>
      </div><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Marketing Automation Without Enterprise Tools</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">ServiceTitan costs $500+/month. HubSpot enterprise is thousands. You don't need either to automate your marketing.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Practical Workflows Using Accessible Tools</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The goal is simple: no lead should fall through the cracks because someone forgot to follow up.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Stack we recommend for sub-$200/month:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">CRM: Housecall Pro or Jobber ($50-100/month). Both handle scheduling, invoicing, and basic automation.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Email automation: Mailchimp or MailerLite free tiers get you started. ConvertKit if you need more sophistication.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Text automation: TextMagic or SimpleTexting ($25-50/month). Critical for lead response speed.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Integration glue: Zapier ($20-50/month). Connects everything.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Lead Follow-Up Sequences That Work</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">When a lead comes in‚Äîwhether from a form, call, or chat‚Äîthis sequence should fire automatically:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">0-5 minutes: Automated text confirming receipt. "Thanks for reaching out about [service]. Someone from our team will contact you within the hour."</p><div class="my-8 p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-[#00D4FF]/10 to-[#00D4FF]/5 border-l-4 border-[#00D4FF] rounded-r-lg">
        <div class="text-4xl lg:text-5xl font-bold text-[#00D4FF] mb-2">70%+</div>
        <p class="text-[#1D1D1F] text-sm lg:text-base m-0">The contractor who calls first gets the job. Speed-to-lead is everything.
</p>
      </div><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">5-15 minutes: Phone call from your team.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Same day: If no answer, follow-up text. "Hi [Name], tried calling about your [service] request. When's a good time to reach you?"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Day 2: Another call attempt + email with helpful information about the service they requested.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Day 4: "Checking in" text. Low pressure.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Day 7: Final "want us to close your request?" message.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This entire sequence can be automated with the tools above. Your job is just making the phone calls. Everything else runs itself.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The mistake I see: contractors who respond to leads within 2-3 days. By then, the customer has already scheduled with two other companies. Speed is your competitive advantage.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Measuring What Matters</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">At this point, you're tracking properly, generating leads from multiple channels, and following up automatically. Now you need to make sense of it all.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">ROI Calculation Framework</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Monthly marketing ROI shouldn't be complicated. Here's the spreadsheet you need:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For each marketing channel, track:
- Total spend (ads + tools + agency fees)
- Leads generated
- Estimates scheduled
- Jobs closed
- Revenue from those jobs</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Then calculate:
- Cost per lead (spend / leads)
- Cost per estimate (spend / estimates)
- Cost per job (spend / jobs)
- Return on investment ((revenue - spend) / spend)</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">A channel with 5x ROI (you spend $1,000, you get $5,000 in revenue) is printing money. A channel with 1.5x ROI barely covers your overhead. A channel below 1x ROI is losing money.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most contractors can't produce these numbers. Which means they can't make informed decisions about where to spend more or less.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Dashboard Setup</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You don't need a fancy BI tool. You need a spreadsheet that answers three questions:</p><ol class="list-decimal pl-6 mb-6 space-y-1"><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Where did our leads come from this month?</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">What did each lead cost us?</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Which channels are generating profitable jobs?</li></ol><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Update it weekly. Review it monthly. Adjust spend quarterly.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">We build these dashboards in Google Sheets connected to call tracking and CRM data via Zapier. Takes about 4 hours to set up, saves thousands in wasted spend.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">The 12-Month Marketing Plan</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If you're starting from scratch or rebuilding a failed marketing effort, here's how to phase it.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Months 1-3: Foundation</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Fix your tracking. This is not optional. You cannot optimize what you cannot measure. GA4 properly configured. Call tracking installed. CRM integrated.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Optimize your Google Business Profile completely. Every field, weekly posts, photo uploads.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Launch LSAs if you haven't. Get through Google's verification process and start generating high-intent leads.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Build or fix your core website pages. Homepage, service pages, contact page. Mobile-optimized, fast-loading, clear CTAs.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Set up basic automation. Lead response texts at minimum.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Months 4-6: Expansion</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Layer in Google Search Ads. Start conservative with exact match keywords. Monitor search terms report weekly to add negatives.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Begin review generation system. Text follow-ups after every job.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Create remaining service and location pages. Expand your SEO footprint.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Test Facebook retargeting. Small budget, focused on people who visited your site but didn't convert.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Months 7-12: Optimization</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Now you have data. Use it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Double down on channels with highest ROI. Cut or reduce channels underperforming.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><a href="/services/cro" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">A/B test landing pages</a>. Try different headlines, different CTAs, different layouts.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Expand geographic targeting if demand supports it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Add seasonal campaigns for predictable spikes (spring for roofing, fall for HVAC).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Build out <a href="/services/content" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">content for longer-tail keywords</a>.
