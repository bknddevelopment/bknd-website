HVAC customers get HVAC emails.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Email is cheap and effective for repeat and referral business. Most contractors don't do it at all.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Marketing Ideas That Waste Money</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Not everything works. Here's what to avoid:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Random social media posting.</strong> Posting to Facebook three times a week with no strategy generates nothing. No one's scrolling Instagram looking for a plumber. Unless you're running paid ads with targeting, organic social is brand maintenance at best.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Cheap SEO packages.</strong> The $299/month SEO agency is submitting your business to spam directories and writing garbage content. You're better off doing nothing. <a href="/services/seo" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">Real SEO</a> takes time and expertise. If the price seems too good to be true, it is.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Lead gen services (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack).</strong> These can work, but understand what you're buying: shared leads sold to 4-5 competitors simultaneously, often with inflated prices. Calculate your actual cost per closed job, not cost per lead. For many contractors, it's astronomical.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Billboards.</strong> Unless you're in a very specific situation (highway in your service area, major brand awareness push), billboards are expensive and untraceable. A $3,000/month billboard could fund your entire digital marketing budget.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Radio/TV for most contractors.</strong> Mass media makes sense when you're trying to reach everyone. Home services usually don't need to reach everyone. They need to reach homeowners in their service area who need their specific service right now. Digital targeting does this better at a fraction of the cost.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Save your money for what works.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Budget Allocation: How to Prioritize</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your marketing budget determines your strategy, not the other way around.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">$1,000/month plan:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">100% to LSAs and Google Business Profile optimization. Don't split this budget. Concentrate on the highest-ROI channel until you've maxed it out.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Do everything else (review generation, photos, referrals, truck wraps) with time, not money. These cost effort, not dollars.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">$3,000/month plan:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">$1,500 LSAs (or until maxed)
$1,000 Google Search Ads (exact/phrase match, strict negative keywords)
$500 Facebook/Instagram retargeting (people who visited your site but didn't convert)</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Plus continued investment in organic: GBP posts, review generation, content for your website.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">$10,000/month plan:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">$3,000 LSAs (likely maxed at this point)
$4,000 Google Search Ads (expanded keywords, geo-targeting tests)
$1,500 Facebook/Instagram (retargeting plus cold audience testing)
$1,500 for professional content, video testimonials, and website improvements</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">At this level, you should be running incrementality tests. Turn channels off temporarily to measure their true contribution. What happens to lead flow when you pause Facebook? Now you know what it's really worth.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Measuring What Matters</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Marketing without measurement is gambling.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Track these numbers:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Leads by source. How many calls, forms, and chats from Google Ads vs LSAs vs organic vs referrals?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Cost per lead by source. Total spend divided by leads.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Estimate rate. What percentage of leads turn into scheduled estimates?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Close rate. What percentage of estimates turn into jobs?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Cost per job by source. This is the number that matters. A channel with expensive leads that close at 80% beats a channel with cheap leads that close at 10%.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Average job value by source. LSA leads might close smaller jobs. Referrals might close bigger ones.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">The monthly report you need:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">One page. Leads, estimates, jobs, revenue, and cost per job by marketing channel. That's it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If your agency can't produce this report, ask why not.
