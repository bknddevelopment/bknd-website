/emergency-roofing/cedar-park</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This captures long-tail searches and gives you more ranking opportunities. A search for "roof repair round rock" will favor a page specifically about roof repair in Round Rock over a generic roof repair page.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Priority: create these for your highest-value services first, then expand based on search volume and competition.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Schema Markup for Local Business</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Schema is structured data that helps Google understand your business. It's invisible to visitors but important for search.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">At minimum, implement LocalBusiness schema on your homepage and location pages. This should include:</p><ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-1"><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Business name</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Address (or service area)</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Phone number</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Hours</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Service type</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Geographic area served</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Aggregate rating (if you have reviews on your site)</li></ul><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Here's what the JSON-LD should look like:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "RoofingContractor",
  "name": "Your Company Name",
  "telephone": "+1-512-555-1234",
  "areaServed": {
    "@type": "GeoCircle",
    "geoMidpoint": {
      "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
      "latitude": 30.2672,
      "longitude": -97.7431
    },
    "geoRadius": "30 mi"
  },
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.8",
    "reviewCount": "127"
  }
}
```</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Use the most specific @type for your business. Google has types for RoofingContractor, Plumber, HVACBusiness, Electrician, and more. Check schema.org for the full list.</p><div class="my-8 p-6 bg-emerald-50 border-l-4 border-emerald-500 rounded-r-lg">
        <div class="flex items-start gap-3">
          <span class="text-emerald-500 text-xl">üí°</span>
          <p class="text-[#1D1D1F] m-0">Test your schema with Google's Rich Results Test. Fix any errors. Invalid schema can hurt more than help.
</p>
        </div>
      </div><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For more help with technical implementation, <a href="/services/seo" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">our SEO services</a> include full schema setup and validation.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Link Building for Contractors</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Links remain one of the strongest ranking factors, but building them for local service businesses works differently than for tech companies or publishers.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Local Opportunities</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your existing business relationships are link opportunities:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">*Suppliers and distributors:* Many have "find a contractor" or dealer locator pages. If you buy from them, ask about getting listed.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">*Manufacturer programs:* GAF, Owens Corning, Carrier, Trane‚Äîmajor manufacturers have contractor networks with website listings.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">*Local business associations:* Chamber of Commerce, Rotary, industry-specific groups. Membership often includes a directory listing.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">*Partners:* Do you refer work to or receive work from other contractors? Link exchanges between complementary businesses (roofer and gutter installer, HVAC and electrician) are natural and valuable.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Sponsorships That Pass Value</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Sponsoring little league teams is great for community relations. But does it help SEO?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Only if you get a link from a website with real authority. The $500 banner at the baseball field does nothing for Google. The $500 sponsorship that gets you a do-follow link from the school district's website? That moves the needle.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Look for sponsorship opportunities that include website mentions: local events, charity runs, school programs, community organizations. Ask specifically about website recognition, not just logo placement on physical materials.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Content That Earns Links Naturally</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Contractors rarely do content marketing well, which means opportunity for those who do.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Create local resources people actually want to link to:</p><ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-1"><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">"How to Prepare Your [City] Home for Hurricane Season"</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">"Average Cost of [Service] in [City]: 2026 Data"</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">"Complete Guide to [City] Building Permits for [Project Type]"</li></ul><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Local journalists and bloggers link to useful local resources. Generic "5 Tips for Hiring a Roofer" content gets ignored.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For a deeper dive into content strategy for contractors, our <a href="/blog/home-services-marketing-guide" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">home services marketing guide</a> covers this extensively.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Tracking Local SEO Success</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You can't improve what you can't measure. Most contractors have no idea if their SEO is working.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Google Business Profile Insights</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your GBP dashboard shows: search queries that triggered your listing, views, direction requests, phone calls, website clicks.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This data is directional, not perfect. Google aggregates and sometimes delays it. But month-over-month trends tell you if you're moving up or down.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Key metrics to track monthly:
- Total searches your profile appeared in
- Direct searches (people searching your business name)
- Discovery searches (people searching for what you offer)
- Phone calls from profile
- Website visits from profile
- Direction requests</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Rank Tracking by Location</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Where you rank depends on where the searcher is located. Someone in downtown Austin sees different results than someone in Round Rock, even for the same search.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Use a rank tracking tool that checks from multiple locations in your service area. BrightLocal, LocalFalcon, and GeoRanker all do this. They'll show you a grid of your rankings across different points in your territory.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Track your main keywords weekly. Look for patterns: strong in some areas, weak in others. This tells you where to focus optimization.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Call Tracking Attribution</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If you're not tracking which marketing generates which phone calls, you're guessing.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Use a call tracking service (CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, WhatConverts) to assign unique phone numbers to different channels. Your GBP listing gets one number. Your website organic traffic gets another (via dynamic insertion). Your paid ads get another.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Now you know: "Last month, 47 calls from GBP, 23 from organic search, 12 from Google Ads."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">One caution: too many tracking numbers can create NAP inconsistency issues. Use dynamic number insertion on your website rather than hard-coding multiple numbers.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">After working with dozens of contractors on local SEO, the same mistakes show up repeatedly.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Hiding the address when customers can visit.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If you have a showroom or office customers can come to, show your address. Hiding it makes you look illegitimate and limits your ranking potential.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Service area too broad or too narrow.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Too broad (entire state) and Google doesn't know where to show you. Too narrow (one zip code) and you miss opportunities. Match it to your actual service willingness.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Keyword stuffing the business name.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Bob's Roofing | Best Austin Roofing Company | Emergency Roof Repair" isn't your business name. Google knows it, and this can get your listing suspended. Use your actual business name only.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Ignoring profile completeness.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Every empty field is a missed opportunity. Fill out services, attributes, hours, Q&A, photos‚Äîall of it.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">One-and-done mindset.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">GBP isn't "set it and forget it." Regular updates, fresh photos, new reviews, and weekly posts signal an active business.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Next Steps</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Local SEO for contractors comes down to a few fundamentals: a fully optimized Google Business Profile, consistent NAP across quality citations, a review generation system, and a website that supports it all.</p><blockquote class="my-8 pl-6 border-l-4 border-[#00D4FF] italic">
