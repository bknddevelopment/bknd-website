Use them.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Legal Considerations</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Recording laws vary by state. The US has two standards:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">One-party consent:</strong> Only one person on the call needs to know it's recorded. You can record without informing the caller. States include Texas, New York, Georgia, and many others.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Two-party consent:</strong> Everyone on the call must consent to recording. States include California, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, and others.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">For contractors:</strong> If you operate in or receive calls from two-party consent states, you need a disclosure. Most platforms let you play an automated message: "This call may be recorded for quality purposes."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Check your state's laws. When in doubt, disclose. The disclosure itself doesn't hurt conversions measurably.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Using Transcriptions for Quality</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Transcriptions turn call recordings into searchable, analyzable text.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Quality assurance:</strong> Search transcriptions for phrases like "not interested," "too expensive," "already hired someone." These indicate lost opportunities. Why are people calling and not converting?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Keyword mining:</strong> What language do callers use? If they say "AC not blowing cold" instead of "AC repair," your ad copy and website content should reflect that language.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Training:</strong> Review transcriptions with your team. How are calls being handled? Are questions answered properly? Is pricing discussed appropriately?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Lead scoring:</strong> Some platforms offer AI-powered scoring that analyzes transcription sentiment and content to automatically score lead quality.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Reporting and Optimization</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Data without action is useless. Here's what to actually do with call tracking data.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Which Sources Drive Calls</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your first report should be simple: calls by source, weekly and monthly.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">| Source | Calls | Cost | Cost/Call |
|--------|-------|------|-----------|
| Google Ads | 45 | $2,500 | $55.56 |
| Organic Search | 32 | $0 | $0.00 |
| Google Business Profile | 28 | $0 | $0.00 |
| Facebook Ads | 12 | $600 | $50.00 |
| Truck Wrap | 8 | $150 | $18.75 |
| Yard Signs | 6 | $50 | $8.33 |</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This immediately shows where your calls come from and what they cost. Most contractors are shocked by how much free traffic (organic, GBP) they're getting—and how much they're paying for paid traffic that isn't their primary call driver.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Call Quality Scoring</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Not all calls are equal. A 20-second wrong number isn't worth the same as a 5-minute conversation that becomes a $10,000 job.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Set up call quality tiers:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-4 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Qualified calls:</strong> 90+ seconds, caller is a potential customer in your service area</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Now calculate cost per QUALIFIED call, not just cost per call. This metric matters for budget decisions.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Cost Per Qualified Call by Source</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The real metric: what do you pay for calls that could become customers?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If Google Ads generates 45 calls but only 25 are qualified, and you spent $2,500, your cost per qualified call is $100—not $55.56.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">If your truck wrap generates 8 calls, 7 qualified, at $150/month, your cost per qualified call is $21.43.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Suddenly the truck wrap looks a lot better than Google Ads for cost efficiency. Maybe you need more trucks. Maybe you need better Google Ads targeting.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This is <a href="/blog/marketing-roi-measurement" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">how you measure marketing ROI</a> properly.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">After setting up call tracking for dozens of contractors, here are the mistakes I see repeatedly.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Not tracking Google Business Profile separately.</strong> GBP calls often outnumber website calls for local businesses. If you're not tracking them separately, you're missing a major chunk of your data.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Using Google's forwarding numbers in call extensions.</strong> Google's numbers give you basic data but don't integrate with recording, transcription, or your CRM. Use your call tracking platform's numbers instead.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Setting pools too small.</strong> A 5-number pool for a site getting 200 daily visitors means heavy overlap and inaccurate keyword attribution. Size your pool to your traffic.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Ignoring offline sources.</strong> Truck wraps, yard signs, and door hangers generate calls. Without dedicated tracking numbers, you'll never know how many.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Not connecting to GA4.</strong> Call data in CallRail and form data in GA4 means no unified view of your conversions. Integrate them.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Forgetting the recording disclosure.</strong> If you serve customers in California, Florida, or other two-party consent states, you need a disclosure. Set it up during initial configuration, not after you've accumulated thousands of uncompliantly recorded calls.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Tracking calls without tracking quality.</strong> 100 calls sounds great until you learn 60 were spam, wrong numbers, or outside your service area. Build quality scoring into your process from day one.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Not integrating with your CRM.</strong> If call data doesn't flow into your CRM, you can't track calls through to closed revenue. You'll know which channels generate calls but not which generate customers.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Next Steps</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Call tracking isn't a one-time setup. It's infrastructure that compounds in value as you accumulate data.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Start with the basics: source-level tracking for your website, Google Business Profile, and offline sources. Get the integrations right from the beginning.
