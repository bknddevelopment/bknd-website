If you'd had our maintenance plan, you'd have gotten 15% off, and we probably would have caught this during your tune-up."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Seasonal campaigns:</strong> Run maintenance plan promotions in shoulder seasons when you have capacity. "Sign up in March, get your first tune-up free" moves the needle.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Email Marketing to Existing Customers</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your customer list is gold. Most contractors ignore it.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Quarterly seasonal emails:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">March: "Summer's coming. Schedule your AC tune-up."
June: "Beat the heat. Our emergency line is available 24/7."
September: "Don't get caught in the cold. Book your furnace inspection."
December: "Thank you for a great year. Here's a special offer for loyal customers."</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Maintenance reminder automation:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">When a customer gets a tune-up, mark the date. 10 months later, send an automated email: "It's almost time for your annual AC tune-up. Click here to schedule."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This is basic email automation. Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or your CRM can handle it. Setup takes an afternoon. The leads it generates are nearly free.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Seasonal Reminder Campaigns</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Text messages work better than email for time-sensitive reminders.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Hi [Name], this is [Company]. Temperatures are dropping fast this week. Want us to schedule your furnace check before the rush? Reply YES for available times."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">We've seen 30%+ response rates on weather-triggered text campaigns. Compare that to 2% email open rates for generic newsletters.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Website Optimization for HVAC</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your website has specific requirements for HVAC that generic web design ignores.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Emergency Contact Prominent</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">When someone's HVAC fails, they're not reading your blog. They're looking for a phone number.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Implementation requirements:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Phone number in the header. Visible without scrolling on every page. Click-to-call enabled on mobile.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Emergency Service" button in the navigation. Bright color. Links to either a phone call or a page with emergency-specific information and a phone number.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Sticky header on mobile that follows the user down the page. The phone number should never disappear.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Live chat or callback widget for users who prefer not to call. Some people want to type "my AC isn't working" rather than explain it verbally.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Financing Options</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">HVAC system replacement is a $8,000-15,000 decision. Most homeowners can't write that check casually.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">What to include:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Dedicated financing page explaining your options (Wells Fargo, GreenSky, Synchrony, whatever you use).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Monthly payment estimates on system replacement pages. "$12,000 system or $189/month for 72 months" is easier to process than a raw price.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Financing available" messaging on service pages. Even if they're looking at repairs now, knowing financing exists plants a seed for future replacement conversations.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Service Area Clarity</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"We serve the greater metro area" is useless. List specific cities.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Create a service area page with every city and neighborhood you cover. This helps SEO and helps users immediately know if you can help them.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Include a map if possible. Visual confirmation matters.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Tracking and Attribution</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Without proper tracking, you're guessing. And your guesses are probably wrong.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Call Tracking for Emergency vs. Planned</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Different call sources require different handling.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Setup structure:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">One tracking number for your Google Ads campaigns.
One tracking number for Google Business Profile.
One tracking number for organic website visitors.
One tracking number for Facebook.
Dynamic number insertion on your website that swaps numbers based on traffic source.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Call categorization:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Tag calls as emergency vs. scheduled during or after the call. Your phone tracking software (CallRail, WhatConverts, CallTrackingMetrics) can add tags.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Over time, you'll see patterns. Maybe Google Ads generates 80% emergency calls. Maybe organic traffic generates more scheduled maintenance requests. That data shapes where you spend.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Lead Source by Season</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Build a simple dashboard that shows lead sources by month.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You'll likely discover that certain channels dominate certain seasons. LSAs might generate 60% of your summer leads but only 30% in spring. Organic might provide consistent year-round traffic while paid varies dramatically.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This informs budget allocation. If organic provides your shoulder-season floor, protect your <a href="/services/seo" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">SEO investment</a>. If LSAs dominate peaks, make sure your profile is optimized before each peak season starts.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Attribution to closed jobs:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The real insight comes from tracking which leads become customers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Integrate your call tracking with your CRM (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber). When a job closes, you should be able to trace it back to the original marketing source.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">A $12,000 system replacement that came from organic search has a different value than a $150 tune-up from a Facebook ad. Your marketing decisions should reflect that difference.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Making This Work</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">HVAC marketing isn't set-it-and-forget-it.
