Not because it's complicated, but because most marketing advice doesn't account for what makes roofing different.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You're selling something invisible until there's a problem. Your customers don't think about you until water is dripping through their ceiling or a hailstorm just passed through. And when they do need you, they're calling three other companies too.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This guide covers 15 strategies that actually work for roofing companies. No theory. Just what generates leads and closes jobs.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Why Roofing Marketing Is Different</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Before we get into tactics, you need to understand why standard marketing advice fails for roofers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Seasonality runs everything.</strong> Spring storms create a surge. Winter kills lead flow in most markets. You can't just "run consistent campaigns" and expect consistent results. Your marketing has to flex with demand.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Storm chasing changed the game.</strong> After major storms, out-of-town crews flood your market. They're knocking doors, running aggressive ads, and competing for the same insurance jobs. You need a strategy that builds local trust faster than they can.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">High ticket means high skepticism.</strong> A $12,000 roof replacement isn't an impulse buy. Homeowners research, compare, and worry about getting scammed. Your marketing needs to build trust before they'll even pick up the phone.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Insurance work adds complexity.</strong> Many roofing jobs involve insurance claims. Your marketing needs to speak to both direct-pay customers and storm damage victims navigating insurance processes. Different audiences, different messages.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Now for the strategies.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">1. Google Business Profile Domination</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your Google Business Profile is the single most important marketing asset you own. When someone searches "roofer near me" or "roof repair [city]", the map pack results get more clicks than anything else.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most roofers set up their profile and forget it. That's a mistake.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Complete every field.</strong> Services, service areas, hours, attributes. Google rewards completeness. Takes 30 minutes.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Add photos weekly.</strong> Before and after shots of actual jobs. Crew photos. Truck photos. Not stock images. Google's algorithm can identify generic stock photos, and so can customers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Post updates weekly.</strong> Nobody reads them, but posting signals to Google that you're an active business. We've tested profiles with weekly posts against identical profiles without. The posting profiles rank higher.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Optimize Q&A.</strong> Add your own questions and answers. "Do you handle insurance claims?" "Yes, we work directly with all major insurance providers for storm damage claims." Free keyword optimization that competitors ignore.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Categories matter.</strong> "Roofing Contractor" as your primary category. Add "Roof Inspection Service" and "Gutter Installation Service" as secondary categories if you offer those services.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This alone can put you in the map pack for high-intent local searches. Free leads, recurring monthly.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">2. Local SEO for "[City] Roofing Contractor"</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Beyond your Google Business Profile, you need your website ranking for local searches. This means <a href="/services/seo" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">building out location and service pages</a> that target specific keywords.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Every city you serve needs its own page. "Roofing Contractor Austin" is different from "Roofing Contractor Round Rock." These pages need:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Unique content.</strong> Not the same page with the city name swapped. Write about that specific area. Mention neighborhoods. Reference local landmarks. Google knows the difference between unique content and find-replace.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Testimonials from that area.</strong> If you have reviews from Round Rock customers, feature them on the Round Rock page.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">NAP consistency.</strong> Your name, address, and phone number must match exactly across your website, Google Business Profile, and every directory you're listed on. "123 Main St" and "123 Main Street" are different to Google.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Schema markup.</strong> LocalBusiness structured data on every location page. This tells Google explicitly that you serve that area. Your developer can implement this in under an hour.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For a roofing company serving 8-10 cities, building out proper location pages can generate 50+ additional organic leads per month. It's tedious work, but the compound returns are significant.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">3. Google Ads for Roofing</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><a href="/services/paid-advertising" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">Google Ads</a> for roofing is expensive. CPCs for "roof replacement" can hit $30-50 in competitive markets. But done right, it's also the highest-intent traffic you can buy.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Start with exact match keywords.</strong> Broad match for roofing is a budget incinerator. Google will happily spend your money on "roofing jobs" (employment seekers), "DIY roof repair" (not hiring anyone), and "roofing materials near me" (contractors, not customers).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Build a negative keyword list immediately.</strong> Add: jobs, employment, career, DIY, how to, supply, supplies, materials, wholesale, contractor license, training. Review your search terms report weekly and add more.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Keyword structure that works:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">High intent (bid aggressively):
- roof replacement + [city]
- emergency roof repair
- storm damage roof repair
- roofing company near me</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Medium intent (bid moderately):
- roof inspection + [city]
- roof leak repair
- new roof cost</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Low intent (bid conservatively or skip):

- how long does a roof last
- signs you need a new roof</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Budget reality:</strong> In most markets, you need $2,000-5,000/month to test Google Ads properly. Below that, you don't have enough data to optimize. If your budget is under $2,000, focus on Local Service Ads and SEO instead.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Landing pages matter.</strong> Don't send paid traffic to your homepage. Build dedicated landing pages for each service. Roof replacement ads go to a roof replacement page with specific pricing information, photos, and a single clear call-to-action.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">4. Local Service Ads: The Roofing Advantage</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Local Service Ads (LSAs) should be your first paid investment. Unlike standard Google Ads, you only pay when someone actually contacts you.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For roofing companies, LSAs have two major advantages.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Trust signals are built in.</strong> The Google Guaranteed badge means Google has verified your insurance, licensing, and background checks. This immediately differentiates you from storm chasers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Pay-per-lead, not pay-per-click.</strong> You're not paying for tire-kickers who clicked an ad and bounced. You're paying for phone calls and messages from people who want to hire a roofer.</p><p class="text-[#1D1D1F] font-semibold mb-4">Getting the most from LSAs:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Response time affects your ranking. Answer calls immediately. Return messages within minutes. Google tracks this.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Reviews heavily influence LSA rankings. More Google reviews with higher ratings means more LSA visibility. This compounds with your organic GBP efforts.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Set your budget by leads, not dollars. Calculate what you can afford to pay per lead based on your close rate and average job value. A $50 lead that closes 20% of the time at $10,000 average job value is wildly profitable.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The limitation: LSA inventory is limited. You can't just spend more to get more leads like traditional ads. But max out LSAs before investing heavily in standard Google Ads.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">5. Review Generation That Actually Works</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Reviews are currency in roofing. They affect your Google rankings, your LSA positioning, and whether a prospect calls you or your competitor.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The mistake most roofers make: they ask for reviews months after the job, if at all.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">The right timing:</strong> Request the review the same day the job completes, while the customer is still happy about their new roof. Not a week later when they've moved on mentally.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">The right method:</strong> Text, not email. A text with a direct link to your Google review page gets 10x the response rate of email. Keep it short: "Hi [Name], thanks for choosing us for your roof. If you have 30 seconds, a Google review helps other homeowners find us: [link]"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">The right volume:</strong> Aim for 5-10 new reviews per month. Consistent review velocity signals to Google that you're an active, legitimate business.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Responding to reviews:</strong> Every single one. Positive reviews get a quick thank you. Negative reviews get a professional response that acknowledges the issue and offers resolution. Future customers read your responses as carefully as the reviews themselves.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Never offer incentives for reviews.</strong> It's against Google's terms, it looks sketchy, and one customer calling you out for it destroys your credibility. A Website That Actually Converts</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your website's job is to convert visitors into phone calls. Everything else is secondary.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most roofing websites fail at this. They're slow. They bury contact information. Customers want to see your work, not stock photos of generic roofs.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Financing information upfront. A $12,000 roof is a lot of money. If your site takes 5 seconds to load on a phone, half your visitors are gone before they see anything.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Trust signals throughout. Licensing info, insurance badges, manufacturer certifications, years in business, BBB rating. These should be visible on every page, not buried on an about page.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">A <a href="/services/cro" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">properly optimized roofing website</a> can double your conversion rate from the same traffic. Show homeowners the actual damage you found.
