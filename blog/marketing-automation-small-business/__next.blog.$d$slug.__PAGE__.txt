These five sequences generate the most ROI.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">1. Speed-to-Lead Follow-Up</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This is the most important automation you will build. Research shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes makes you 21 times more likely to qualify them compared to waiting 30 minutes.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most service businesses respond in hours. Some respond in days. Many never respond at all.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Here is the sequence that works:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Minute 0:</strong> Lead comes in (form submission, phone call, or ad).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Minute 1:</strong> Automated text message. "Hi [Name], this is [Company]. We got your request for [service]. What is the best time to discuss?"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Minute 2:</strong> Automated email with more detail. Include your phone number, brief company intro, and what to expect next.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Minute 3:</strong> Internal notification to your team. Slack message, email alert, or CRM task assignment. Someone needs to call this lead now.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Minute 15:</strong> If no response to the text, send a follow-up. "Just wanted to make sure you got my message. We typically respond within a few hours."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Hour 1:</strong> If still no response, another touchpoint. This one can be a voicemail drop if your system supports it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 1, 3, 7:</strong> Continued email sequence with value-add content relevant to their inquiry. Not "just checking in" messages. Actual information about the service they requested.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This sequence runs automatically for every single lead. No manual intervention required until someone responds.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">2. Review Request Automation</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You need reviews. You will not remember to ask for them consistently. Automate it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Trigger:</strong> Job marked complete in your CRM.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Timing:</strong> Wait 2-4 hours. Long enough for them to experience the work, short enough that satisfaction is still fresh.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Channel:</strong> Text message first. Email as backup.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Message:</strong> "Hi [Name], thanks for choosing [Company] for your [service]. If you were happy with our work, would you take 30 seconds to leave us a Google review? [direct link]"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">That direct link matters. Do not send them to Google and make them search for you. Generate a direct review link from your Google Business Profile.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For more detail on making <a href="/services/analytics" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">analytics and tracking work together</a>, including review attribution, that guide covers the technical setup.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Follow-up:</strong> If no review after 3 days, send one more message. After that, stop. Do not badger people.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Some businesses segment this: only send review requests to customers who rated their experience 4+ stars in a post-job survey. This protects against negative reviews while still generating volume.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">3. Appointment Reminders</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">No-shows cost money. Every missed appointment is lost revenue plus wasted drive time.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">24 hours before:</strong> Text and email reminder with appointment details. Include what to expect, how to prepare, and a clear way to reschedule.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">2 hours before:</strong> Quick text reminder. "We will see you at 2pm today. Reply C to confirm or R to reschedule."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">On the way:</strong> "Our technician is on the way and will arrive in approximately 30 minutes."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This sequence reduces no-shows by 30-50% in my experience. It also reduces "I forgot" phone calls that waste your office staff's time.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">4. Quote Follow-Up Sequence</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You give an estimate. The customer says they need to think about it. Then silence.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Most contractors follow up once, maybe twice, then give up. The customer goes with whoever was most persistent.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Here is a better approach:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 1 after estimate:</strong> Thank you email with estimate summary and answers to common questions.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 3:</strong> Text message checking in. "Hi [Name], wanted to see if you had any questions about the estimate we sent. Happy to clarify anything."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 7:</strong> Email with additional value. Could be a case study, financing options, or warranty information relevant to their project.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 14:</strong> Phone call (human, not automated) if the lead is qualified and the job is worth it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Day 30:</strong> Final email. "Your estimate for [project] is still valid. If you decide to move forward, we would love to help."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The key insight: most estimates are not lost on day one. They are lost in the follow-up desert between day 3 and day 14. Automation keeps you present during that decision window.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">5. Past Customer Reactivation</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Your best leads are people who already hired you once. They know you. They trust you. They just need a reminder.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">For HVAC and seasonal services:</strong> Automated maintenance reminders.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Hi [Name], it has been 6 months since we serviced your AC. Most systems need annual maintenance to stay efficient. Want to schedule your tune-up? [booking link]"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">For one-time services (roofing, remodeling):</strong> Periodic check-ins with referral asks.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Hi [Name], we installed your roof 2 years ago. How is it holding up? If you know anyone who needs roofing work, we would appreciate the referral. We offer $X for successful referrals."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">For all services:</strong> Annual holiday or seasonal touchpoint. Not a sales pitch. Just staying top of mind.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Happy holidays from [Company]. Thanks for being a customer. If you need anything in the new year, we are here."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This is not aggressive. It is maintenance. One or two touchpoints per year keeps you in their memory when they need you again or when a friend asks for a recommendation.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Lead Follow-Up Automation: The Deep Dive</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Let me break down the speed-to-lead sequence in more detail because this is where most service businesses lose the most money.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">The Channels</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">SMS is king for immediate response.</strong> 98% of text messages are read within 3 minutes. Email sits unread for hours. Phone calls go to voicemail. Text gets through.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Email handles detail and documentation.</strong> Attach estimates, share links, provide information that does not fit in a text.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Phone remains critical for closing.</strong> Automation gets them engaged. A human closes the deal.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">The Workflow Architecture</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Here is how I structure the workflow in Go High Level (similar logic applies in other platforms):</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Trigger:</strong> New contact created from any source (web form, Facebook lead ad, manual entry, phone call transcription).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 1:</strong> Wait 1 minute. (Gives time for any data syncing.)</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 2:</strong> Send SMS template. Dynamic fields pull their name and inquiry type.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 3:</strong> Wait 2 minutes.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 4:</strong> Send email template.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 5:</strong> Create task for sales team with high priority.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 6:</strong> Wait 1 hour.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 7:</strong> Condition check: Did they reply? (Check for inbound SMS or email.)</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">If yes:</strong> Stop automation, notify team for human follow-up.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">If no:</strong> Send follow-up SMS.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 8:</strong> Wait 1 day.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 9:</strong> Another condition check. Reply or callback?</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">If yes:</strong> Stop and notify.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">If no:</strong> Send Day 1 email (value content, not another "just following up").</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This continues through Day 7, gradually decreasing intensity and switching from "did you get my message" to "here is helpful information."</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Message Templates That Convert</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Bad follow-up text: "Hi, this is ABC Plumbing following up on your inquiry. Please call us back at your earliest convenience."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">That reads like a robot wrote it. Because a robot did.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Good follow-up text: "Hey [Name], it's Mike from ABC Plumbing. Saw you need help with [specific issue]. What's the best time to call you today?"</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Personal. Specific. Asks a concrete question.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">For email, the same principle applies. Do not write corporate-speak. Write like a human who wants to help solve their problem.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Review Generation That Scales</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Let me expand on the review automation because <a href="/blog/hvac-marketing" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">HVAC marketing</a> and other service industries live and die by their review count.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">The Technical Setup</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 1:</strong> Create a Google review direct link. Go to your Google Business Profile, click "Ask for reviews," and copy that link. Or construct it manually: https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=[YOUR_PLACE_ID]</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 2:</strong> Set up the trigger in your CRM. "Job status changed to Complete" or "Invoice marked Paid" both work.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 3:</strong> Add a delay. I use 2-4 hours for most services. For emergency services where relief is immediate, 1 hour can work.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 4:</strong> Send the text. Keep it short. Include the link. Make it easy.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Step 5:</strong> Send backup email 24 hours later if no review detected. (Some platforms can detect when a review is posted and stop the sequence.)</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Handling Negative Experiences</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The last thing you want is to automate asking an unhappy customer for a review.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Two approaches:</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Post-job survey first:</strong> Before asking for a review, send a one-question survey. "How would you rate your experience? 1-5 stars." Only trigger the review request for 4-5 star responses. Route 1-3 star responses to a manager for personal outreach.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Technician feedback:</strong> Let your technician flag difficult jobs or unhappy customers. If flagged, skip the automated review request.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Either approach is better than blindly asking everyone.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Seasonal Campaign Automation</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><a href="/blog/home-services-marketing-guide" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">Home services marketing</a> is inherently seasonal. HVAC has summer and winter peaks. Roofing follows storm seasons. Landscaping follows growing seasons.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Manual campaign management means scrambling every season transition. Automation means campaigns run themselves.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Spring HVAC Example</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">January 15:</strong> Segment your list for "Residential AC customers."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">February 1:</strong> First email. "Spring is coming. When did you last service your AC? Schedule your tune-up before the rush." Include early-bird pricing if you offer it.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">February 15:</strong> SMS to non-openers. Same message, different channel.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">March 1:</strong> Second email with urgency. "Our March schedule is 40% booked. Lock in your tune-up time now."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">March 15:</strong> Final push to remaining non-booked customers.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This runs every year automatically. Adjust the content once, and the sequence handles itself.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Storm Season for Roofers</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Trigger:</strong> Weather alert in your service area. (Some automation platforms integrate with weather APIs.)</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Immediate response:</strong> Email and SMS to your customer list. "Severe weather expected in [area]. If you experience any damage, we're here to help. Save our number: [phone]."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Post-storm (48 hours later):</strong> "The storm has passed. We're offering free inspections this week for any homeowners concerned about damage. [Booking link]."</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This positions you as responsive and helpful while generating legitimate leads.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Integration: Making Systems Talk</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Stand-alone tools are useless if they do not share data. This is where <a href="/services/analytics" class="text-[#00D4FF] hover:underline">proper analytics setup</a> separates professionals from amateurs.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">The Integration Stack</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Zapier or Make:</strong> These no-code platforms connect almost anything. They work on a trigger-action model.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Example: When a new contact is created in Jobber (trigger), add them to a Mailchimp audience (action), send a welcome email (action), and add a row to a Google Sheet for reporting (action).</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Native integrations:</strong> Many platforms now connect directly. Go High Level integrates with Facebook Lead Ads natively. Housecall Pro connects to QuickBooks. Check what your tools already support before adding middleware.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Webhooks for custom connections:</strong> If you have a developer (or access to one), webhooks handle anything Zapier cannot. Your website form can POST data directly to your CRM. Your CRM can notify your analytics platform when a job closes.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Common Integration Patterns</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Lead capture to CRM:</strong> Website form submits to your CRM, creating a contact and triggering the speed-to-lead sequence.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">CRM to email platform:</strong> New contacts sync to your email marketing tool for newsletter and campaign sends.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Job completion to review request:</strong> Closed job triggers the review automation sequence.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Call tracking to CRM:</strong> Inbound calls create or update contact records with call recordings attached.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">CRM to analytics:</strong> Closed revenue data passes back to Google Analytics and ad platforms for accurate ROI tracking.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This last one is critical. Without it, you optimize for leads. With it, you optimize for actual revenue.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Measuring Automation ROI</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Do not set up automation and forget it. Measure what matters.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Metrics to Track</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Speed to lead:</strong> Average time from lead submission to first touchpoint. Target: under 5 minutes.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Response rate:</strong> Percentage of leads who reply to automated sequences. Benchmark: 15-25% for SMS, 5-10% for email.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Review request conversion:</strong> Percentage of completed jobs that result in reviews. Benchmark: 10-20%.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Reactivation revenue:</strong> Revenue generated from past customer campaigns. Track this separately from new customer revenue.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed"><strong class="text-[#1D1D1F]">Automation-influenced deals:</strong> Deals where automated touchpoints occurred before human conversion. This shows automation's contribution to pipeline.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">The Reporting Setup</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Create a monthly automation report with:</p><ul class="list-disc pl-6 mb-6 space-y-1"><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Total leads processed through automation</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Average response time</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Lead-to-appointment rate</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Reviews generated</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Reactivation campaign revenue</li><li class="mb-3 text-[#86868B]">Any automation failures or errors</li></ul><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">This report takes 30 minutes to build once and 10 minutes to review monthly. It is how you know your system is working.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">After building automation for dozens of service businesses, I see the same mistakes repeatedly.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Mistake 1: Over-Automation</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Sending 15 emails in 7 days does not make you persistent. It makes you spam.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The fix: Map your sequence to actual customer decision timelines. Emergency services need fast, intense follow-up. Major purchases need patient, value-based nurturing.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Mistake 2: Generic Messages</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">"Just following up" and "wanted to touch base" signal that you have nothing to say.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The fix: Every automated message should provide value or ask a specific question. If you cannot justify why a message exists, delete it.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Mistake 3: No Human Handoff</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Automation warms leads. Humans close deals. If your sequence has no point where a human takes over, you will lose conversions.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The fix: Build clear triggers for human intervention. Hot lead indicators (replied, clicked multiple emails, high-value service) should notify your team immediately.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Mistake 4: Set and Forget</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">Automation is not a crockpot. You cannot set it and walk away forever.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The fix: Monthly review of metrics and message performance. Quarterly updates to copy and sequences. Annual overhaul to align with business changes.</p><h3 class="text-xl lg:text-2xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-10 mb-4">Mistake 5: Ignoring Compliance</h3><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">SMS marketing has legal requirements. TCPA violations can cost $500-1,500 per message.</p><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">The fix: Get explicit opt-in before texting. Include opt-out instructions. Honor unsubscribes immediately. When in doubt, consult a lawyer.</p><h2 class="text-2xl lg:text-3xl font-semibold text-[#1D1D1F] mt-12 mb-6">Getting Started</h2><p class="text-[#86868B] mb-6 leading-relaxed">You do not need to build everything at once.
