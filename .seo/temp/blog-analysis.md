## Writing Style and Tone

### Voice Characteristics

1. **Direct and authoritative**: "Let me be direct about what is happening."
2. **Data-driven**: Heavy use of statistics and specific numbers
3. **Action-oriented**: Every article includes "What to do now" sections
4. **No fluff**: Articles get to the point quickly without excessive preamble
5. **Professional but accessible**: Technical topics explained clearly for business owners

### Structural Patterns

- **Opening hook**: Bold statement or statistic that captures attention
- **Custom callout blocks**: :::stat, :::key, :::warning, :::tip, :::pullquote
- **Stat callouts**: Large numbers (e.g., "800M", "95%") with context
- **H2 sections**: Major topic divisions
- **H3 subsections**: Detailed breakdowns
- **FAQ sections**: At the end of most articles
- **CTAs**: Subtle but consistent links to /contact and service pages

### Formatting Conventions

- Bold text for emphasis on key terms
- Bullet lists for tactics and implementation steps
- Numbered lists for sequential processes
- Statistics prominently featured with visual callouts
- Read time: typically 7-18 minutes (substantial, authoritative content)
- Author: Charwin Vanryck deGroot

### Tone Examples

- "This is not a 'wait and see' situation."
- "The question for marketers is not whether to engage, but how quickly..."
- "Let me clear up a common misconception..."
- "Here is what most SEO coverage gets wrong..."
- "The smart play is to treat this as you would have treated early Google Ads..."
